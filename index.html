Theory for cases:

  1. Case Interview Secrets by Victor Cheng
  2. LOMS podcasts by Victor Cheng - MUST
  3. Case in Point by Mark Cosentino (a little bit outdated, but is still considered as a classic book)
  4. The Pyramid Principle by Barbara Minto (for structuring skills)
  5. Crafting cases online course (relatively new) - may be useful after you learn the basics

Cases for the practice you can find on/in:

  1. preplounge website
  2. consultingcase101 website
  3. Different case books (Harvard, Wharton, LBS etc)
  4. How would you move Mount Fuji - for brainteasers (if you apply not only to big-3, where this type of questions is almost never asked)

For development of business-sense:

  1. articles in The Economist, Harvard Business Review, local papers etc
  2. big-3 firms mobile apps (for reports, market outlooks, articles etc)

For fit:

  1. PARADE method by Victor Cheng
  2. How to interview like a top MBA

Hope someone else will add their fav materials and we would be able to make a useful list :)

Oh, and I forgot to add one more website I use frequently often (caseinterview.com) - it is actually Victor Cheng’s website. There are different useful things there, but my favorite one is the tool for training math:

https://www.caseinterview.com/math/mathtest.php

It allows to choose one of two modes: precise or estimation (+-20%); allows to choose which type of math problems you want to practice (+, -, *, /, % - or random), allows to choose a difficulty level and also allows to compare your results with Victor Cheng’s results, all users average or with top-20% users average :)

And I hope everyone here knows how important are your mental math skills during both the test and interview
